National Youth Parliament Speech

Union Budget 2026: Strengthening the Youth Path Towards Viksit Bharat 2047

“उत्तिष्ठत जाग्रत प्राप्य वरान्निबोधत।” Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is achieved — Katha Upanishad Respected Chairperson, Honourable Members of this august house, Today’s discussion is not merely about a financial statement presented before the Parliament. Today’s discussion is about the future of India. A Union Budget is often seen as a document full of numbers, allocations, fiscal targets, and policy announcements. But in reality, a national budget is far more meaningful than that. It is the expression of a nation’s priorities and the roadmap of its collective aspirations. Every line written in a budget carries with it the silent hopes of millions of citizens — the dream of a student striving for quality education, the determination of a young entrepreneur trying to build something meaningful, and the ambition of countless young Indians who wish to contribute to the progress of this nation. And therefore, when we discuss the Union Budget 2026, we are not merely analyzing an economic policy. We are discussing how India will shape the destiny of its youth and how that youth will shape the destiny of India.

India’s Youth: The Nation’s Greatest Strength

Chairperson, India today stands at a historic moment in its journey. With nearly two-thirds of our population below the age of 35, India possesses one of the largest youth populations in the world. This demographic strength is not just a statistic — it is a once-in-a-century opportunity. History tells us that whenever a nation successfully empowers its youth, it experiences unprecedented growth and transformation. But history also warns us that demographic advantage can turn into demographic pressure if opportunities are not created in time. Therefore, the real question before us is not whether India has the youth power. The real question is: Are we creating the ecosystem where this youth power can transform into national power? And the answer to that question lies significantly in the Union Budget.

The Union Budget as an Investment in Youth

Chairperson, A budget is not merely an exercise in distributing resources. It is an investment in the future. When the government allocates funds for education, skill development, innovation, and entrepreneurship, it is essentially investing in the potential of young Indians. For a young student studying in a rural school, the budget determines whether there will be better classrooms, modern laboratories, digital learning tools, and trained teachers. For a young graduate entering the workforce, the budget determines whether there will be industries, research centers, and employment opportunities that allow their talent to flourish. For a young entrepreneur with an innovative idea, the budget decides whether there will be access to credit, startup support, and a policy environment that encourages risk-taking and innovation. In that sense, the Union Budget becomes a bridge between aspiration and opportunity.

Education, Skills, and Innovation

Chairperson, If India truly wants to become a Viksit Bharat by 2047, the foundation must be built on knowledge, skills, and innovation. A developed nation is not defined merely by its infrastructure or GDP figures. A developed nation is defined by the capabilities of its citizens. That is why investments in education and skill development are not just social spending — they are strategic national investments. When we strengthen schools, universities, research institutions, and technical training programs, we are building the intellectual capital of the nation. When we promote startups, digital innovation, artificial intelligence, and emerging technologies, we are ensuring that India is not merely participating in the global economy — India is shaping the future of the global economy. And when young Indians are empowered with the right skills and opportunities, they do not merely seek jobs — they create jobs.

Entrepreneurship and Economic Participation

Chairperson, The youth of India today are not waiting for opportunities to come to them. They are creating opportunities themselves. Across the country, from small towns to major cities, young Indians are launching startups, building innovative technologies, and solving real-world problems. But innovation does not grow in isolation. It requires supportive policies, financial access, and an enabling ecosystem. That is why the Union Budget plays such a crucial role. Through investments in startup ecosystems, research funding, digital infrastructure, and MSME support, the budget creates an environment where young minds can transform ideas into enterprises. And when young entrepreneurs succeed, they do not just create wealth for themselves — they create employment, innovation, and economic growth for the entire nation.

Youth and the Vision of Viksit Bharat 2047

Chairperson, When we speak about Viksit Bharat 2047, we are speaking about a future where India is economically strong, technologically advanced, socially inclusive, and globally respected. But such a transformation cannot be achieved by policies alone. It will be achieved by the millions of young Indians who will innovate, lead, create, and build the India of tomorrow. The Union Budget therefore becomes more than an economic document. It becomes a national commitment to empower the youth who will carry this country forward for the next quarter century. Because the journey towards a developed India will not be written merely in government reports. It will be written in classrooms where young minds are inspired, in laboratories where discoveries are made, in startups where new ideas take shape, and in villages and cities where young citizens work with determination to improve their communities.

Conclusion

Chairperson, If India truly wishes to achieve the vision of Viksit Bharat 2047, then the most important investment we must make today is the investment in our youth. When young Indians are educated, skilled, confident, and empowered, they become the engine of national transformation. And when the Union Budget prioritizes education, innovation, entrepreneurship, and opportunity, it does not merely allocate funds — it builds the foundation of a stronger and more prosperous India. Therefore, the Union Budget 2026 must be seen as a strategic investment in India’s greatest asset — its youth. Because if we empower our youth today, the dream of Viksit Bharat 2047 will not remain a distant vision. It will become the shared reality of a confident, capable, and forward-looking nation. And when the youth of India move forward with determination and purpose, the rise of India becomes not just possible — it becomes inevitable. Jai Hind. Jai Bharat.
