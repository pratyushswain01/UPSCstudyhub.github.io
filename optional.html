PSIR Detailed Syllabus

  • Paper 1: Political Theory and Indian Politics
    • Political Theory: Concepts, ideologies, social justice, liberty, equality
    • Indian Government and Politics: Constitution, federalism, democracy, electoral politics
  • Paper 2: International Relations and Comparative Politics
    • International Relations: Theories, UN, India and global order
    • Comparative Politics: State, society, political economy, development and change

Recommended Books for PSIR

  • Introduction to Political Theory - O.P. Gauba
  • Indian Government and Politics - B.L. Fadia
  • Politics in India - Rajni Kothari
  • Indian Constitution - M. Laxmikanth
  • International Relations Since 1945 - P. Hill
  • Politics in Developing Nations - Peter Burnell

Preparation Tips for PSIR

  • Focus on understanding political theory fundamentals and major ideologies.
  • Stay updated with recent developments in Indian politics and governance.
  • Develop strong conceptual clarity on the Indian Constitution and polity.
  • Follow standard newspapers and journals for current international relations.
  • Practice analytical writing and discuss polity issues with peers.
  • Refer to previous years' question papers for exam pattern understanding.

Additional Suggestions for PSIR

  • Use flowcharts and diagrams to remember political processes.
  • Watch online lectures & debates on political concepts to improve understanding.
  • Join discussion groups or forums specialized in PSIR optional.
  • Build notes progressively and revise them regularly.

Geography Detailed Syllabus

  • Paper 1: Physical Geography
    • Geomorphology, Climatology, Oceanography
    • Biogeography, Environmental Geography
    • Maps, Remote Sensing, and GIS
  • Paper 2: Human and Economic Geography
    • Population, Settlements, Regional Development
    • Agriculture, Industry, Resource Geography
    • Transport, Trade, and Economic Development

Recommended Books for Geography

  • Physical Geography - Savindra Singh
  • Human Geography - Majid Husain
  • Geo-Book 1 & 2 - NCERT Class 11 & 12
  • Indian Geography - Majid Husain
  • Economic and Social Geography of India - Majid Husain
  • Fundamentals of Remote Sensing and GIS - Anji Reddy

Preparation Tips for Geography

  • Develop strong basics from NCERT textbooks of Classes 11 and 12.
  • Use maps and atlas extensively for better geographic orientation.
  • Keep abreast of current environmental issues and geopolitics.
  • Practice diagrammatic representation and labeling for exams.
  • Use online GIS and remote sensing tutorials for practical knowledge.
  • Revise important geographical terms and processes frequently.

Additional Suggestions for Geography

  • Watch topic-wise video lectures for complex physical and human geography concepts.
  • Create mind maps and charts for revision and better retention.
  • Visit geographical locations or national parks to gain practical insight.
  • Follow geography current affairs for the dynamic parts like disasters and environment.
