PSIR (Political Science & International Relations)

Syllabus

The detailed syllabus for PSIR is as follows:

  • Paper 1: Political Theory and Indian Politics
  • Paper 2: Comparative Politics and International Relations

Books and References

  • 1. M. Laxmikanth - Indian Polity
  • 2. Andrew Heywood - Politics
  • 3. V. R. Mehta - Foundations of Indian Political Thought
  • 4. David Held - Introduction to International Relations
  • 5. Ramesh Thakur - International Relations

Tips and Suggestions

  • Focus on both theory and current affairs.
  • Use newspapers and journals like The Hindu and Frontline for current developments.
  • Practice previous years' papers for better time management.
  • Integrate case studies from international politics in your answers.

Geography

Syllabus

The detailed syllabus for Geography is as follows:

  • Paper 1: Physical Geography
  • Paper 2: Human Geography

Books and References

  • 1. G.C. Leong - Physical Geography
  • 2. Majid Husain - Geography of India
  • 3. Savindra Singh - Geographical Thought
  • 4. R. K. Gupta - Physical Geography
  • 5. C.D. Deshpande - Geography of India (Oxford)

Tips and Suggestions

  • Practice diagram-based questions for both physical and human geography.
  • Be thorough with maps and their interpretation.
  • Keep revising the core concepts of physical geography.
  • Link your knowledge to current environmental and developmental issues.
