📋 GS Paper I - Complete Syllabus

Civil Services (Preliminary) Examination - General Studies Paper I

Syllabus Overview

  • Current events of national and international importance
  • History of India and Indian National Movement
  • Indian and World Geography - Physical, Social, Economic Geography
  • Indian Polity and Governance - Constitution, Political System, Panchayati Raj, Public Policy, Rights Issues
  • Economic and Social Development - Sustainable Development, Poverty, Inclusion, Demographics, Social Sector initiatives
  • General issues on Environmental Ecology, Bio-diversity and Climate Change
  • General Science

Exam Pattern

  • Total Questions: 100 Questions
  • Maximum Marks: 200 Marks (Each question carries 2 marks)
  • Duration: 2 Hours (120 Minutes)
  • Negative Marking: 1/3rd penalty (0.66 marks deducted for each wrong answer)
  • Type: Objective Type (Multiple Choice Questions)

Preparation Tips

  • Focus on NCERT textbooks (Class 6-12) for core concepts
  • Read newspapers daily for current affairs (The Hindu, Indian Express)
  • Practice Previous Year Questions (PYQs) extensively
  • Take regular mock tests to improve time management
  • Make concise notes and revise regularly
  • Focus on quality over quantity - understand concepts deeply

📋 CSAT Paper II - Complete Syllabus

Civil Services (Preliminary) Examination - CSAT (Qualifying Paper)

Syllabus Overview

  • Comprehension
  • Interpersonal skills including communication skills
  • Logical reasoning and analytical ability
  • Decision-making and problem solving
  • General mental ability
  • Basic numeracy (numbers and their relations, orders of magnitude, etc.) - Class X level
  • Data interpretation (charts, graphs, tables, data sufficiency etc.) - Class X level

Exam Pattern

  • Total Questions: 80 Questions
  • Maximum Marks: 200 Marks (Each question carries 2.5 marks)
  • Duration: 2 Hours (120 Minutes)
  • Negative Marking: 1/3rd penalty (0.83 marks deducted for each wrong answer)
  • Qualifying Nature: Minimum 33% marks (66.66 marks) required to qualify
  • Important: Marks obtained in CSAT are NOT counted for ranking. It's only a qualifying paper.

Preparation Tips

  • Focus on accuracy over speed - you only need 33% to qualify
  • Practice comprehension passages daily - they carry maximum weightage
  • Revise basic mathematics from Class 6-10 NCERT
  • Develop shortcuts for quick calculations
  • Practice data interpretation from newspapers and reports
  • Attempt easier questions first to secure qualifying marks
  • Don't ignore CSAT - many candidates have failed only due to CSAT
