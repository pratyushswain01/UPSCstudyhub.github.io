Regional Governance & Statutory Bodies

North Eastern Council
(NEC)

The North Eastern Council (NEC), established in 1971, serves as the premier agency for the social and economic development of India’s northeastern region. It marked a pivotal shift toward focused, coordinated efforts to accelerate the growth of the eight "Ashta Lakshmi" states.

Establishment & Significance

  • Legal Foundation: Established by the North Eastern Council Act, 1971. It is a Statutory Body (not constitutional).
  • Coverage: Encompasses eight states: Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Manipur, Mizoram, Sikkim, Nagaland, and Tripura.
  • Role: Acts as the nodal agency for regional planning, infrastructure investment, and social empowerment in the North Eastern Region (NER).

Composition of the NEC

PositionMember Details
Ex-Officio ChairmanThe Union Home Minister.
Ex-Officio Vice-ChairmanMinister of State (IC) of the Ministry of Development of North Eastern Region (DoNER).
Permanent MembersGovernors and Chief Ministers of the eight North Eastern States.
Nominated MembersThree members nominated by the President of India.

Key Objectives

  • Balanced Development: Promoting integrated and sustainable growth across all NER states while ensuring equitable resource distribution.
  • Infrastructure Focus: Formulating plans for regional transportation, communication, energy, and essential services.
  • Regional Integration: Fostering seamless connectivity and economic linkages between the NER and mainland India.
  • Institutions of Importance: Supporting the establishment of Institutes of National Importance to build the region's intellectual capital.

Primary Functions

Planning & Coordination

Developing Unified Regional Plans encompassing matters of common importance, project priorities, and locations.

Financial Advocacy

Recommending the financial assistance required for regional projects to the Central Government for fund allocation.

Resource Management

Prioritizing inter-state transport, flood control, and common power projects to address shared regional needs.

Security Evaluation

Reviewing State measures for maintaining public order and security, offering recommendations for peace and stability.

NER Vision 2020: Milestones

This visionary document aimed at bridging the development gap. Key outcomes include:

  • Telecom: Considerable improvement in connectivity and digital reach across the NER.
  • Physical Infra: Major implementation of projects in Roads, Railways, Airways, and Power sectors.
  • GDP Growth: Reached an average GDP growth of 9.8% during the 12th Five Year Plan (2012-17).

Master Conclusion

The North Eastern Council has been the backbone of regional harmony and development for over five decades. By addressing inter-state border disputes (like the Assam-Mizoram joint statement) and fostering decentralized dialogue, the NEC bridges gaps and ensures the North East remains an equal partner in India’s progress.
