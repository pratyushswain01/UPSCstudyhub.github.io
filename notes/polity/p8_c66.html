Lokpal and Lokayuktas in India

Back to Main Menu

“The countrymen should not lose this spirit, this is our fight against corruption” – Anna Hazare

Evolution, Advocacy, and Legislative Milestones

  • In 1809, the institution of ombudsman was inaugurated officially in Sweden.
  • In India, the concept was first proposed by law minister Ashok Kumar Sen in the early 1960s.
  • The terms Lokpal and Lokayukta were coined by Dr. L. M. Singhvi.
  • In 1966, the First ARC recommended two independent authorities at central and state levels. In 2005, the Second ARC chaired by Veerappa Moily recommended setting up the office without delay.
  • The “India Against Corruption Movement” led by Anna Hazare resulted in the passing of the Lokpal and Lokayuktas Bill, 2013. It received Presidential assent on 1 January 2014 and came into force on 16 January 2014.

Global Context: The Ombudsman Framework

The success of democracy depends on the redressal of citizens’ grievances. The Scandinavian institution of Ombudsman (1809) handles:

  • Abuse of administrative discretion (misuse of power).
  • Maladministration (inefficiency).
  • Administrative corruption (bribery).
  • Nepotism and Discourtesy.

Features include independence from executive, Suo moto power, uninterrupted access to files, and simple/speedy methods.

Composition and Tenure of Lokpal

Lokpal is a multi-member body (1 Chairperson + maximum 8 members).

  • Chairperson: Former CJI, former SC Judge, or eminent person with 25 years of expertise in anti-corruption, finance, or law.
  • Members: 50% must be judicial members. Minimum 50% must be from SC/ST/OBC/Minorities and women.
  • Judicial Member: Former SC Judge or former Chief Justice of a High Court.
  • Tenure: 5 years or until the age of 70 years.

Appointment Process

Appointed by the President on the recommendation of a Selection Committee:

Sl No. Selection Committee Members
1Prime Minister (Chairperson)
2Speaker of Lok Sabha
3Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha
4Chief Justice of India or a Judge nominated by him/her
5One eminent jurist

Former SC judge Pinaki Chandra Ghose is India’s first Lokpal.

Jurisdiction and Exemptions

  • Scope: Includes Prime Minister, Ministers, MPs, Groups A, B, C, and D officers.
  • PM Exemption: Allegations related to international relations, security, public order, atomic energy, and space are exempt. Inquiry requires a full bench and 2/3rds approval. Probes are held in camera.
  • Parliamentary Immunity: No jurisdiction over things said or votes given in Parliament.

Powers and Functions

  • Superintendence: Has power over CBI; investigating officers in referred cases cannot be transferred without Lokpal's approval.
  • Wings: Has an Inquiry Wing (Civil Court powers) and a Prosecution Wing.
  • Asset Seizure: Can confiscate assets procured via corruption and suspend public servants.

Working Timeline:

  • Establish prima facie case after seeking explanation.
  • Preliminary Enquiry report: Within 60 days.
  • Preliminary investigation: Normally within 90 days.
  • Special Court trials: Must complete within one year (Total period max 2 years).

Difference between Lokpal and Lokayukta

Aspect Lokpal Lokayukta
Jurisdiction National level. State level.
Scope Central government officials. State government officials.
Appointment President of India. Governor of the respective state.
Composition 1 Chairperson + up to 8 members. Varies (Lokayukta and Upa Lokayukta).
Accountability Accountable to the President. Accountable to the Governor.

Way Forward

  • Streamline complaint procedures emphasizing substance over formality.
  • Empower Suo Motu authority for proactive investigations.
  • Ensure adequate staffing, infrastructure, and financial resources.
  • Insulate institutions from political interference to bolster autonomy.
  • Encourage states to expedite Lokayukta establishment for local redressal.

Conclusion

Despite challenges like delayed appointments, Lokpal and Lokayuktas represent a significant step toward ethical governance. Strengthening their operational capacity and independence will foster public trust and contribute to a more accountable and corruption-free India.
